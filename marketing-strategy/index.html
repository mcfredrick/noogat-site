Core Marketing Strategy

"The Search Engine for Your Thoughts"

01

Target Market

Primary — The ADHD Professional

r/ADHD (3M members) Developers Designers Founders Consultants

People with ADHD — diagnosed or self-identified — in cognitively demanding roles. Their minds move fast and generate ideas constantly, but the friction between "thought occurs" and "thought captured" is where ideas die. They're not looking for another note-taking app. They've already tried Notion, Apple Notes, Bear, and Obsidian and found them all too slow.

This audience is vocal, tight-knit, and evangelical about tools that actually work for them. One authentic founder post in r/ADHD can drive hundreds of downloads. They are the launch wedge.

Secondary — The Busy Thinker

Commuters Deep workers Parents Professionals in flow state

Non-ADHD people who are simply overloaded. They've experienced the frustration of a good idea evaporating mid-meeting or mid-drive. They find Noogat via App Store search and convert because the use case is immediately obvious.

Grow with community first (ADHD), scale with search second (Busy Thinker). The ADHD community provides trust and word-of-mouth; the broader audience provides organic search volume.

02

Value Proposition

The Problem

Your brain generates its best ideas at the worst moments — mid-meeting, mid-conversation, mid-drive, mid-focus. The standard options are bad: follow the out thought and lose your focus, or ignore it and lose the idea. Most people have learned to just lose the idea.

"In thoughts" are what you're focused on. "Out thoughts" are everything else your brain throws at you while you're trying to concentrate. For the ADHD brain especially, an uncaptured out thought doesn't just disappear — it competes for attention and derails the task at hand.

The Insight

That tradeoff is false. What people actually need is a way to make the out thought feel safe — captured, organized, findable — in under three seconds, without breaking what they were doing. When a thought feels safe, your brain lets go of it. You stay focused and you keep the idea. Have your cake and eat it too.

What Noogat Does

Say "Hey Siri, I have an Idea" without touching your phone. The thought is tagged automatically by AI, stored searchably, and waiting for you when you're ready. You stay focused. You keep the idea. No tradeoff.

Why the Second Half Matters

Capture without retrieval is just a graveyard. AI auto-tagging and semantic search mean you can find "that pricing idea from last Tuesday while driving" three weeks later — without remembering any specific words. That's what makes the "you'll find it later" promise credible. And it's what separates Noogat from a voice memo.

05

Freelancers, Budget & What You Can Do Yourself

For a bootstrap solo launch, the goal is high learning value and high ROI — not outsourcing everything. Here's the priority order.

Hire First — Highest ROI

Copywriter with ADHD / Mental Health Community Experience

Everything else — ASO, social media, paid ads — depends on having the right words first. Good Noogat copy isn't about listing features. It's about articulating the "out thought" insight in a way that makes someone say "that's exactly what happens to me."

Deliverables to ask for: App Store listing copy (title, subtitle, description, keywords), landing page copy, 3–5 social post templates.

Where to find them: Contra, Toptal, or post in ADHD/productivity creator communities asking for a copywriter who's personally in the space.

$500–$1,500 one-time
Hire Second — Month 2+

ASO (App Store Optimization) Specialist

App Store Optimization is technical but learnable. Once you have real copy, real reviews, and a conversion rate to work from, a one-time ASO audit can meaningfully improve organic installs. Don't do this before you have data — there's nothing to optimize yet.

Deliverables to ask for: Keyword audit, competitor keyword gaps, metadata recommendations, screenshot copy review.

$300–$600 one-time
Skip For Now

Social Media Management, Paid Ads (Meta/Google/TikTok)

Poor ROI for an indie productivity app pre-traction. Social management burns budget without a proven message. Meta/Google ads are crushed by iOS privacy changes. Only revisit Apple Search Ads after 50+ reviews and a baseline conversion rate — you need data before you can calculate whether paid CAC is profitable.

What You Can Do Yourself (Free, High-Impact)

  • →
    Post in r/ADHD — your single highest-leverage launch action. Not an ad. A founder story: "I have ADHD, I kept losing ideas mid-meeting, so I built this." r/ADHD has 3M members and responds extremely well to authentic posts from people in the community.
  • →
    Post on Indie Hackers — building in public. Write up your launch story and early numbers honestly. The IH audience overlaps heavily with your target user. Other indie founders become evangelists.
  • →
    App Store keyword research. Use AppFollow or Sensor Tower's free tier to find what ADHD productivity seekers actually search. Takes an afternoon and compounds forever. Do this before hiring an ASO specialist — you'll get more out of them if you understand the basics.
  • →
    Personal outreach to 20–50 ADHD professionals. DM people in r/ADHD who've posted about losing thoughts or struggling with note-taking. Offer a TestFlight link. These early users become your most loyal evangelists.
  • →
    Record a 60-second demo video. Scene 1: locked phone → "Hey Siri, I have an Idea" → "got it!" Scene 2: colorful tagged list. Scene 3: semantic search finding an old idea. Shoot on iPhone, edit in CapCut. This video is the foundation for every future marketing asset.
